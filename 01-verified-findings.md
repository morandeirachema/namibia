# Namibia 4x4 self-drive, Oct–Dec 2026 — Verified findings (pass 1)

Two adults, 14 days, round trip from A Coruña. Research date: 2026-07-16.

Everything below survived 3-vote adversarial verification against primary sources.
Claims that were **refuted** are listed at the bottom — several are widely repeated online
and would have cost real money if believed.

---

## 1. The 14/15 November price cliff — the single biggest cost lever

Every rental company checked drops from peak to low season in mid-November.
The **same camping-equipped Toyota Hilux double cab, 14 days**:

| Start date | Asco rate | 14-day total |
|---|---|---|
| October 2026 (band 15/08–14/11) | €179/day | **~€2,506** |
| After 15 Nov 2026 (band 15/11–14/03) | €117/day | **~€1,638** |

**Difference: ~€870.** Namibia2Go confirms the same pattern independently — its high
season ends 31 Oct 2026 (N$3,520/day), dropping to N$2,700/day from 1 Nov 2026
(a 23.3% saving).

Notes:
- Asco's post-15/11 band runs to 14/03/27 and sits within ~6% of the annual January minimum.
- Namibian rentals commonly **pro-rate across season boundaries** — the "October costs
  more" penalty only applies fully to a trip wholly inside the peak band.
- The cliff exists *because conditions change* on that date. The environmental side of that
  trade-off (rain, heat, game viewing) is **not yet researched** — see gaps below.

## 2. Camping kit is bundled, not billed separately

Asco's camping rate includes roof tent, fridge, tables, chairs, bedding, BBQ grill,
pots/pans and kitchen tools at **no separate hire line**.

The rate table proves it: same vehicle, same season (15/03–14/07/26, 6–15 days) —
standard Hilux **€120/day** vs camping-equipped **€127/day**. The €7/day delta *is* the kit.
There is no "add camping kit" SKU on the standard-4x4 page at all.

Included: 15% VAT, CDW, second spare wheel, compressor, emergency tools, airport
transfers, one additional driver, unlimited mileage.

Excluded from **both** scenarios: excess reduction, €250 non-refundable booking deposit,
fuel, out-of-hours/weekend/public-holiday collection fees, young-driver surcharge,
one-way and cross-border fees. Campsite fees are still a separate line in the camping scenario.

- https://www.ascocarhire.com/4x4-with-camping-1-2-persons.html
- https://www.ascocarhire.com/4x4-standard.html

## 3. Insurance is the real financial risk — not the daily rate

**Every tier below the top one excludes exactly the damage you're likely to suffer on gravel.**

### Asco (4 tiers)
| Tier | Cost | Excess |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | €0/day | N$40,000 |
| Reduced Excess 1 | €8/day | N$20,000 |
| Reduced Excess 2 | €15/day | N$5,000 |
| **Super Cover** | **€25/day** | **N$0** |

Verbatim: *"Reduced Excess 1 and 2 do not cover damages to tires, windows, damages to the
undercarriage of the vehicle, and Single-Vehicle-Accidents"* — the base tier excludes the same four.

**Super Cover** adds windows, **one (1)** tyre, single-vehicle accidents (negligence excluded),
and undercarriage damage *"excluding Kaokoveld and Damaraland Area"* — a void that hits the
Twyfelfontein leg directly. Requires a rental of **more than 10 days** (a 14-day trip qualifies).

Asco's own warning, worth quoting: Super Cover *"cannot be compared with the European
Vollkasko or All Risk insurance."*

### Savanna (camping-equipped Hilux/Ranger)
| Tier | Cost | Excess |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | — | N$35,000 |
| Option 1 | N$100/day | N$17,500 |
| Option 2 | N$150/day | N$8,500 |
| Option 3 | N$220/day | N$2,500 |
| **Option 4** | **N$450/day** | not published |

Tyre + windscreen cover is **N$250/day extra** on Standard and Options 1–3; included in Option 4.
RE1–4 require a minimum 8-day rental.

**Tyre caps matter:** Asco covers **one** tyre, Savanna **two**. Any further puncture across
2,500–3,500 km of gravel is on you.

### The single-vehicle accident trap
Under Savanna's terms a rollover or loss of control with no third party exposes you to
**N$165,000 plus all recovery costs** — and this is *not* removed by the lower tiers.
Verbatim: *"Also not when you tried to avoid hitting an animal crossing the road."*

⚠️ Savanna's own pages are **internally contradictory** on Option 4 — it lists
"Vehicle role over/overturning" as covered while elsewhere excluding single-vehicle accidents
from RE4 too. **Get written clarification before booking.**

- https://www.ascocarhire.com/insurance.html
- https://www.savannacarhire.com.na/reduced-excess-insurance
- https://www.savannacarhire.com.na/rental-conditions

## 4. The 80 km/h gravel limit is enforced by black box

Asco imposes **80 km/h on all gravel roads** — 20 km/h below the national limit — and
*"if speed regulations are broken, your insurance and excess will lapse and become invalid."*

The FAQ kills the "unenforceable boilerplate" assumption:
*"The cars are equipped with a black box that tracks both speed and location. In the event
of an accident, the data will be read and analyzed."*

Industry-standard, not an Asco quirk — Namibia Car Rental imposes the same. TripAdvisor
threads complain about black-box-based claim denials, which confirms it is actively applied.

**Planning consequence:** every driving-time estimate must be computed at 80 km/h on gravel,
not 100. That compresses the achievable daily range by ~20% versus most online itineraries.

- https://www.ascocarhire.com/insurance.html
- https://www.ascocarhire.com/faq.html

## 5. Namibia2Go — better insurance terms (medium confidence)

Bundles **zero-excess cover**, tyre & glass waiver, theft & collision waiver and unlimited km
into the headline rate. No deposit required. Corroborated by an independent booking agent
(madbookings.com), so not purely vendor self-description.

Caveats: zero excess applies *"unless negligent driving is proven"* — a gravel rollover deemed
negligent could still generate liability. Water Damage Waiver, Border Fees, fines and lost-key
call-out are excluded.

Rated **medium** confidence only because it materially outperforms Asco and Savanna on terms,
which warrants scepticism until confirmed at booking.

- https://namibia2go.com/rack-rates
- https://www.madbookings.com/namibia-namibia2go.html

## 6. Visa — Spanish nationals DO need one (since 1 April 2025)

- **Fee: N$1,600 (~€78–82)**, via **e-visa online** (issued ~24h, must be **printed and signed**
  in front of the immigration officer) or visa on arrival.
- Spain is one of 33 affected states. Fee rose from N$1,200 on 1 Apr 2025.
- Visa on arrival was *expanded* to 36 more countries in Sept 2025 — not being withdrawn.
- ⚠️ A Cabinet-approved but **ungazetted N$2,000 manual-VoA surcharge** is pending — re-check
  nearer departure. **Prefer the e-visa.**

**Also required at entry:**
- Passport valid **6 months beyond return date**, with **3 blank pages**
- Return/onward ticket
- International health insurance covering all medical costs **including repatriation**
- Proof of accommodation
- Proof of funds — Spanish MFA benchmarks **N$1,200 (~€60) per day** ≈ **~€1,680 for two over 14 days**
  (single-sourced and hedged as *"como referencia"*; FCDO says only "sufficient funds")

Spain's MFA advisory was last updated 26 May 2026 — ~7 weeks old.

- https://www.exteriores.gob.es/es/ServiciosAlCiudadano/Paginas/Detalle-recomendaciones-de-viaje.aspx?trc=Namibia
- https://www.gov.uk/foreign-travel-advice/namibia/entry-requirements

## 7. Park fees rose 80–100% on 1 April 2026

**Do not use any figure you find online from the 2021 schedule.**

- Premium parks (Etosha, Namib-Naukluft/Sossusvlei, Ai-Ais/Fish River Canyon):
  **N$150 → ~N$280 per international adult per day** (N$140 entrance + N$140 conservation)
- Standard parks: N$100 → N$200. Under-8s exempt.

**Mechanism (verified verbatim from the MEFT PDF):** *"Fees are valid for 24 hours period
beginning at the time of entry, per person, per park... Minimum amount payable is for 24 hours
(1 day), and thereafter in units of 24 hours."* A 3-day Etosha stay is charged **3 times**.

**Budget at: ~N$280/adult/day + ~N$60/vehicle ≈ N$620/day for two adults + car**, at each of
Etosha, Sossusvlei and Fish River Canyon.

Minor ambiguity: 24h-from-entry vs gate practice — some operators bill per calendar day and
would charge 4 units for a 3-night stay.

- https://www.meft.gov.na/files/downloads/543_Park%20Entrance%20and%20Conservation%20Fees.PDF
- https://namibian.org/blog/namibia-raises-park-fees-by-80-to-100-percent

## 8. Flights — thin so far

Ethiopian Airlines is a genuine **single-carrier** option Madrid → Windhoek via Addis Ababa
(both legs on ET metal), ~13h flying plus layover. Its Addis–Porto service routes via Madrid,
making **Porto a second ET-served option** from Galicia.

The companion **price** claim (from €1,029 return) was **refuted 1–2** and is not reliable.
Fares are still being researched.

## Source-quality caveat

9 of 16 verified claims are **vendor self-published** rate cards and T&Cs. That is the correct
primary source for "what does this company charge / what do its terms say", and several
disclose *against* their own interest (excess liabilities, exclusions, speed limits), which
raises trust. But it means the rental section reports what vendors **promise**, not what they
**do**. TripAdvisor threads document disputed excess deductions.
